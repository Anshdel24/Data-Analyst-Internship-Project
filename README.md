# HR Analytics: Predicting Employee Attrition

## 📌 Project Overview
This project focuses on identifying the key drivers behind employee resignation (attrition) and building a predictive machine learning model to forecast future turnover risk. Utilizing a synthesized dataset of 1,200 employee records, the project uncovers data-driven insights to help HR teams optimize retention strategies.

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies Used
* **Language:** Python 3.x
* **Data Manipulation:** Pandas, NumPy
* **Data Visualization:** Seaborn, Matplotlib
* **Machine Learning:** Scikit-Learn (Decision Tree Classifier)

---

## 📊 Key Analytical Insights
1. **The Overwork Factor:** Employees logging more than 260 average monthly hours showed a significantly higher propensity to leave the company.
2. **Compensation Imbalances:** Attrition rates were starkly higher in the **Low** salary band compared to the **Medium** and **High** bands.
3. **Departmental Trends:** The Sales and Engineering departments experienced the highest overall volumes of staff turnover.

---

## 🤖 Model Performance Evaluation
A **Decision Tree Classifier** was trained to identify at-risk employees based on their satisfaction metrics, workload, tenure, and department classification. 

* **Classification Accuracy:** Consistently achieves high predictive accuracy.
* **Key Risk Identifiers:** Low job satisfaction levels (< 0.4) combined with a lack of promotion over the last 5 years were flagged as the primary catalysts for attrition.
